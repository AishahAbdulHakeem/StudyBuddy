# StudyBuddy

StudyBuddy is a student-matching app concept designed to help Cornell students find study partners based on shared courses, academic interests, and profile information.

The project focuses on a common student problem: finding classmates to study with can be difficult, especially in large courses or when students do not already know people in the class. StudyBuddy makes academic connection-building more approachable by organizing potential study partners around shared classes and learning goals.

## Project Overview

StudyBuddy is designed as a lightweight academic networking tool for students. Instead of relying only on group chats, office hours, or already knowing someone in a course, students can use StudyBuddy to discover peers who are taking the same classes and are also interested in studying together.

## Problem Statement

Students often want study partners, but the process is informal and inconsistent. Common barriers include:

- Not knowing who else is actively looking for study partners
- Large courses where classmates are hard to identify
- Lack of centralized spaces for course-based academic matching
- Difficulty finding people with similar schedules, goals, or study preferences

StudyBuddy addresses this by making study partner discovery more structured, searchable, and student-centered.

## Core Features

- Browse student profiles
- Match with students taking the same classes
- Search or filter by course
- View academic interests and study preferences
- Support course-based study group discovery
- Create a more approachable way for students to connect academically

## User Flow

```text
Student creates or views profile
        ↓
Selects courses or academic interests
        ↓
Browses classmates with shared courses
        ↓
Finds potential study partners
        ↓
Connects for studying or group formation
```
